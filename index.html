Katherine Johnson: The Human Computer

A Timeline of Katherine Johnson's Life and Achievements

  • 1918: Born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia.
  • 1937: Graduates from West Virginia State College at age 18 with degrees in mathematics and French.
  • 1953: Hired by the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA), which later became NASA, to work as a "human computer."
  • 1961: Calculates the trajectory for Alan Shepard, the first American in space.
  • 1962: John Glenn specifically requests that Johnson re-check the electronic computer's calculations for his orbital mission, highlighting the high regard for her accuracy.
  • 1969: Calculates the trajectory for the Apollo 11 flight to the Moon.
  • 1986: Retires from NASA after 33 years of service.
  • 2015: Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama.
  • 2016: The movie *Hidden Figures* is released, chronicling her life and the contributions of other Black female mathematicians at NASA.
  • 2020: Passes away at the age of 101.

"We will always have STEM with us. Some things will never change." - Katherine Johnson
